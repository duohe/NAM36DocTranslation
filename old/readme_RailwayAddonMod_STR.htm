Rail Addon Mod - Single Track Rail Set 1 by the RAM Team
The Single Track Rail (STR) NAM plugin represents an entirely new addition to SC4 that allows the user to create single track railway systems in their quads and cities, either on a standalone basis or integrated into the games existing dual track rail networks. It has been intensively developed and tested over the past six months, and this Set 1 provides not just the basics, but a fully functional transit network component that is completely integrated into the game.

Regarding the Traffic Plugins in the NAM, they will work exactly the same as if it were the Maxis dual track rail. Please keep in mind that STR tracks are pathed in both ways, so trains might "collide" when they approach from different directions. However, they will simply go through each other, as they were ghost trains. ;-) For busier rail lines, use the regular dual track rail to avoid this phenomenon.
 How to use the STR in the game

The STR consists of three types of content: starter pieces, draggable trackage, and ploppable trackage. Each of these is covered below. In creating a STR rail line, you will begin your line by using one of the two starter pieces and then extend it by using the familiar "click and drag" technique employed for laying other SC4 transport networks such as highways and roads. Finally, you can superdetail the default draggable trackage with ploppable items such as long radius curves and switches.

Starter Puzzle Pieces

Starter pieces do just what they are called - they allow you to start laying your STR line. If you've used the Street Addon Mod (SAM), you are familiar with the concept - you plop the two-tile starter section and then drag the desired network away from one end of it. The starter pieces: one for orthogonal and one for diagonal trackage, are the first two selections in your STR submenu. The ortho starter shows up immediately, and the diag starter is just a push of the [TAB] button away.

You can rotate each piece in one of four different directions (orthogonal: 0, 90, 180, and 270 degrees, diagonal: 45, 135, 225, and 315 degrees) by pushing the [Home] key to rotate the piece counterclockwise and the [End] key to rotate it clockwise. By doing this, you have taken your first step toward including STR in your game.

Draggable Trackage

You then select the game's track laying tool by selecting the Rail tool from the menu, or pressing the [T] key, click and hold the left mouse button with the cursor positioned on the end of the starter piece that is not "dulled," and drag STR track in the direction determined by the orientation of the starter piece.

Releasing the left mouse button "lays" the track.

You then demolish with the "bulldoze" tool (shortcut: press the [B] key) the dulled starter piece tile.

This leaves with with a stretch of STR trackage that you can drag from either end.

Default switches are easy to create, either by click-dragging one STR segment that is at a 45 degree angle to another STR line onto the second line, or by click-dragging from the existing STR line onto the the segment.

Ploppable Trackage

An extensive set of ploppable trackage, also known as puzzle pieces, has been developed and released in STR Set 1 to supplement the default draggable content. Several of these items are also necessary in order to be able to connect your STR line to your existing dual track lines.

Ploppable STR trackage is used just like the FARR - you select the piece you want, position it and plop it. The wide radius curves are about four times as gentle as the default curves, and really add that RL look to your STR lines.

The STR to dual track transitions give you a different way of integrating STR into your existing rail system. Switches have been made so that you can position them by placing them over existing orthogonal trackage. Finally, the filler pieces allow those "hard to convert" spaces to be addressed.

 Compatibility

This download is only compatible with SimCity 4 "Rush Hour" or SimCity 4 Deluxe.
 Installation

Auto Installation

Double-click the installer file to start the installation. The program will guide you through the process. You can change the default installation path, but we strongly suggest to keep the standard folder in order to make future updates and additions easier.

Mac Users

A separate ZIP version of the Single Track Rail files is available as well.

Un-Installing

To un-install the files, simply bulldoze every Single Track Rail item from this download in your cities and delete the files from the plugins folder afterwards.
